Compute the following numbers and enter them in the fields. All numbers are in base 10. You are assembling the letter indexes for a standard substitution cipher. You may want to do this on a computer and save the numbers in a document---they won't save otherwise! The boxes below are just to help you organize the numbers.

Once you have all the numbers, test them with the instructions at the bottom of the page. When all the numbers are correct, use them to assemble a substitution cipher table like this: 

input letters:  | Q (17) | J (10) | M (13) ...
output letters: | A (1)  | B (2)  | C (3)  ...

The input letters are in the order shown below and they map to letters of the alphabet. The output letters need to be in ABC order.

Verify your work by calculating a SHA256 checksum on the numbers by going to https://defuse.ca/checksums.htm and entering the numbers you found into the text box in this format (all on one line, one space between all numbers):

1 2 3 4

Do not press enter and make sure that there are no characters at the end of the number list. Click "Calculate checksums..." and compare the "sha256" checksum to 90e6c8ea6a16b6376ebb4468f7333bdd27127af48dfbcb387b0a501a8ea45480. If it matches, congratulations! You have solved step 2 and can press "Next" below to move to the next step. If the hashes do not match, go back and make sure all your numbers are correct.
